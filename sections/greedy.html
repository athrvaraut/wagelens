Algorithm 02 — Greedy Approach

Tax Bracket Optimizer

Greedy algorithms make the locally optimal choice at each stage with the hope of finding a global optimum. In tax computation, we greedily fill each slab from lowest to highest rate.

⏱ Time: O(k) where k = number of tax brackets  |  Space: O(1)
01
Greedy Choice
Always fill the lowest tax slab first before moving to higher ones
02
Optimal Substructure
Tax on each slab is independent — optimal local = optimal global
03
No Backtracking
Once a slab is filled, we never revisit — greedy commits to each choice

Why Greedy Works for Tax Computation

Greedy Choice Property: The tax system has independent slabs — the tax on ₹3L–₹7L does not depend on decisions made above ₹7L. Each greedy choice is safe.

Optimal Substructure: The optimal tax for income X = optimal tax for the first slab + optimal tax for remainder. This recursive property confirms greedy correctness.

Proof by Exchange Argument: Suppose we tried to pay a higher slab first. We'd end up paying more tax on the same income — contradicting optimality. Therefore greedy (lowest slab first) is always optimal.
